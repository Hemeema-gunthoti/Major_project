What is Steganography?

Imagine a Secret Hidden in Plain Sight

Steganography is the art of hiding information in plain sight. It’s like slipping a secret note into a photo, a song, or even a video—so it looks completely normal to everyone else.

How Does It Work?

- **Hiding Information:** Small, subtle changes are made to files (like images or audio) that are invisible to the human eye or ear.
- **Revealing the Message:** Special tools or software are used to extract the hidden information.

Everyday Example

Think of it like writing a secret message with invisible ink. To everyone else, the page looks blank, but with the right technique, the message appears.

Where is it Used?

  • Digital Watermarks: Protect copyrights by embedding invisible marks in images or videos.
  • Secure Communication: Send private messages without raising suspicion.
  • Data Storage: Store sensitive data discreetly.
